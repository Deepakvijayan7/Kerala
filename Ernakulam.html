Ernakulam

Ernakulam District is situated almost at the middle of Kerala State and on the coast of the Arabian Sea. Ernakulam District is Commercial capital of Kerala as it is the major business hub of Kerala State and the highest revenue yielding district in the state . It is the third most populous district in Kerala,after Malappuram and Thiruvananthapuram. It is surrounded by Thrissur District, Idukki District, Alappuzha and Kottayam districts and also by Arabian sea to west.Ernakulam district also hosts the highest number of international and domestic tourists in Kerala state.This district was formed in 1958 by carving out regions from Thrissur and Kottayam district.The district is bounded by a 46.2 km. coastline of the Arabian Sea on the west.

Cherai Beach

Cherai Beach also called as The Princes of Arabian Sea is a long beach located about 25 km from Cochin in the Vypin Island. The Cherai beach a calm and peaceful beach ideal for swimming and sunbathing.Tourist can enjoy the boat rides, beach volleyball, football, or take a brisk walk along the shore.Cherai Beach have a coast line of 15 km its deep and shallowness attracts lots of tourists and swimmer to this beach.

Locals come here often for picnic and relaxing. Locals enjoy kite flying here. It is one of the most beautiful beaches of Kerala which shows exclusive view of sea and backwaters with lush green coconut palms and paddy fields in its proximity. Water boats are available at the beach on rent, tourist can enjoy the boat ride on this scenic beach.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport about 20 km.
- Ernakulam Railway Station is located about 40 km from the beach.
- Cochin is is the nearest city to Cherai beach which is very well connected by roads to other cities of Kerala. From Cochin there are buses to the beach at regular intervals .

Willingdon Island

Willingdon Island is an island that is the part of Cochin City located in Ernakulam District of Kerala India. It is port of Kochi and Naval Base for Southern Naval Command of India. It was named after Lord Willingdon that governor of Madras at British Times. The island is an artificial man-made island created from the sand dug out while deepening Kochi port.

Robert Bristow 1936 was the chief engineer and architect of this island , it was established by soil dredged out while deepening the Vembanad Lake to accommodate the new Kochi Port. Robert Bristow, the chief protagonist and engineer for the project, owned the first building on the island. It is also a major tourist center of Kerala and houses some of the district’s best hotels, commercial and industrial offices.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport About 32 Km away.
- Ernakulam Junction About 6 Km away. It is a major Railway Station of Kerala and is connected to most cities of Kerala and other states.
- Willingdon Island is connected to Kochi’s mainland by road and the railway Venduruthy Bridge.Kochi is well connected by road from all parts of the state as well as other major cities. To reach Willingdon Island you can either take the road journey or you can go by ferries . Ferries here run at regular interval .

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi is a town located in the Cochin City of Kerala India. It is in the south-west of the mainland of Cochin surrounded by water region all over . Fort Kochi glorifies the Portuguese period of Kerala and is a great historic town . Mattancherry is located nearby the town . Fort Kochi shows the charm of Portuguese era in its architecture and surroundings. The streets will show reunite you with the ancient times of Kerala . There are buildings designed in the Portuguese style architecture and many monuments are located here in Fort Kochi.

Fort Kochi have various Dutch and Portuguese monuments like Santa Cruz Basilica , St Fracis Church , Dutch Palace. Here you will also find huge fishing nets also called as Chinese Fishing Nets .

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport About 45 Km away.
- Ernakulam Junction About 13 Km away. It is a major Railway Station of Kerala and is connected to most cities of Kerala and other states.
- Kochi is well connected by road from all parts of the state as well as other major cities. To reach Fort Kochi you can either take the road journey or you can go by ferries . Ferries here run at regular interval .
