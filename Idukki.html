Idukki

Idukki means a place with a deep gorge. River Periyar flowed through a canyon between two rocky mountains. Kurava and Kurathy. The legend told from generation of the ancient Adivasi Muthuvan tribe here is that Sita, during her forest sojourn with Rama, bathed in the canyon, and on seeing an Adivasi couple – Kuravan and Kurathy watching her naked beauty, she cursed them into two rocky mountains.Agriculture is the main occupation of the people in the District. Dairy is the main supplementary source of income of the farmers in the district.The economy of Idukki is predominantly agricultural, Cardamom, tea, tapioca, rice, pepper, rubber, coconut, sugarcane, coffee, are canut, ginger, lemon grass and vegetables are the most important agricultural produces of the district.About 66% of the State’s Power needs come from the Hydroelectric Power Projects in Idukki.This beautiful High range district of Kerala is geographically known for its mountainous hills and dense forests.

Thekkady

Thekkady is a small place in Periyar national park located in Idukki District of Kerala India.Thekkady is enchanting as the road winds through tranquil countryside, rich plantations & thick jungles.The sanctuary, 60 km from Idukki,190 km from Cochin and 114 km away from Kottayam is open throughout the year, but the best season for a visit is from September to March. The sanctuary is centered around the large (24 sq km) artificial lake formed by a dam across the Periyar river. It is one of the famous holiday destinations in India.

Thekkady is considered a heaven for natural spices such as black pepper, cardamom, cinnamon and clove.Periyar National park is a famous tourist destination of Kerala.

Best time to visit
Thekkady is located in lap of natural beauty surrounded by greenery all over.The climate here is temperature varying between 21degree C to 27degreeC with minimum seasonal variation.Best season for a visit is from September to March.

Eravikulam National Park

Eravikulam National Park is a protected located in the High Ranges of the Southern Western Ghats in the Devikolam Taluk of Idukki district in Kerala State.It spread over an area of about 97 Km2 of rolling grasslands.The Eravikulam National park was declared a national park in the year of 1978.It was declared a Sanctuary in the year 1975 with the intention of protecting the indigenous population of Nilgiri Tahr.The main body of the park consists of a high rolling hill plateau with a base elevation of about 2,000 m. The average annual rainfall is about 3000mm.The Best reason to visit the Eravikulam National park in the month of January-February.The deeper valleys of the forest are extensively forested.A well formed security wing is working to protect the park day and night.

How to reach?
- Eravikulam bus stand is nearly 15 Km north of Munnar, Kochi bus station is major bus stand located 135 Km away and Kottayam is about 148 Km by Road.
- Aluva Railway Station is the nearest railway station located about 120 Km from Munnar and Coimbatore Airport is located about 165 Km in Tamil Nadu.
- Cochin International Airport is nearest located about 148km and Coimbatore International Airport is located about 175 Km respectively.

Ramakalmedu

Ramakalmedu is a town that is located in the mountainous regions of the state of Kerala and it is a picturesque place. The town is located in the Western Ghats and is located at a height of around 3500 feet above sea level. This mountain is named after the impressions of the feet of Lord Rama have been found on the rock surface. This legend has been mentioned in the epic Ramayana too. One can also visit frog rock, turtle rock and kuravan and kurathi – the highest twin statue of the state Kerala located in the town. The place is one of the windiest places in Asia.

Rolling green hills and the fresh mountain air make Ramakalmedu and enchanting retreat. The hilltop also offers a panoramic view of Tamil Nadu towns of Cumbum, Theni, Kombe, Thevaram, Uthamapalayam, Bodinaykannor and Vaiga. The sight is even wonderful at dusk when all these towns lighted.

How to reach?
- For Idukki it is located about 30 km away. It can be reached traveling along State Highway 19.
- Madurai International Airport in Tamil Naidu is the nearest airport to this place about 140 km from here . Cochin International Airport is located about 190 km from here.
- Changanacherry Railway Station is the nearest airport located about 90 km from Ramakalmedu.

Powerhouse Waterfall

Powerhouse Waterfall is located on the way to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary about 18 km from Munnar Kerala. The waterfall is located about 2000 meters above sea level. The roar of water splashing can be heard from distance away. Surrounded by mountain ranges of Western Ghats it gives beautiful breathtaking view. It is a prt of Falls river of Kerala.

Powerhouse waterfall is named after the the old power house located adjacent to it. The water of the fall is believed to come from the Devikulum located near Munnar and is said to have healing powers. It is a popular tourist place that is one the way to Munnar and Thekkady and tourist come here to relax and enjoy the serene beauty of this place.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport also known as Nedumbassery Airport is located 110Km far from Munnar by road.
- Railway station of Aluva, Ernakulam, Madurai which are located 110km, 130km and 135 km respectively are the nearest railway stations from Munnar.
- The waterfall can be easily reached by Munnar. Munnar is well connected by road as it is top destination of Kerala. Reaching Munnar is easy and via Munnar one can reach the Powerhouse Waterfall.
