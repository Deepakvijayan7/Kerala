Alappuzha

District is one of the district of Kerala State of India which is a widely known tourist destination, and is well known for its coir factories.Alappuzha is also called as Alleppey . Alappuzha is strongly connected by waterways to various other parts of Kerala, including the famous tourist destination, Kumarakom. It is called as Venice of East due ti its extensive water connecting canal to cities .The entire area of the district lies in the low land and the midland divisions, and is the only district in Kerala having no area under the high lands. Kuttanad, the rice bowl of Kerala is in Alappuzha district and this is the only region in the state, lying below the sea level. Alappuzha is the most important center in the State for coir industry.Alappuzha, came into being as a district, in the political map of Kerala on the 27th of August, 1957. Before the formation of the district, a major part of this area was of Kollam district and the rest, of Kottayam district.

Pathiramanal

Pathiramanal in Muhamma panchayat of Alappuzha district is a small island under the State Tourism Department. The island boasts of its scenic beauty surrounded by lakes at both sides . Pathiramanal is home to some of the very rare migratory bird from around the world. Pathiramanal is located in the famous Vembanad Lake of Kerala.

Pathiramanal is paradise for Bird Watchers and Nature Lovers , you will find around 91 local species of birds and 50 migratory birds. Pintail Ducks, Common Teal, Night Heron, Cormorant, Darter, Indian Shag, Purple Heron, gulls, terns, large egrets, Intermediate Egret, Cattle Egret, Indian Pond Heron, Little Egret, Pheasant-tailed and Bronze-winged Jacanas, Stork-billed Kingfisher, Watercock, Whistling Duck, Cotton Pygmy-Goose, Little Cormorant and Whiskered Tern are some of the famous species that can be spotted easily.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport about 85 Km away. Trivandrum Airport is about 150 Km from Here.
- Alappuzha Railway Station is the nearest Station from Here.
- It can be reached only via boats there are no road connection to here. It takes hour and half drive by motor boat or 30 minutes by speed boat from Alappuzha town. From Kumarakom it can be reached via boats in 40-50 minutes approx.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach is located in Alapuzzha District of Kerala. The beach is about 11 km from Alleppey town. Alleppey known for its breathtaking backwaters is famous tourist destination of Kerala that attracts hoards of tourist from all over the world. Marari Beach is in the Mararikulam a small fishing village in the outskirts of Alappuzha. Marari beach is not a famous beach , local people come here to enjoy and relax . Its a picnic spot for the local residence. The sea water is clean and ideal for swimming.

It is peaceful beach and one can see fisherman all around and their lifestyle. The Village and the Beach gives the glimpse of traditional and ancient Kerala. There are only few resorts around the beach.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport is ;located about 86 km away. Trivandrum Airport is located about 157 km from Alleppey
- Alappuzha is well connected by rail network. One can reach here from many cities like Cochin, Thiruvananthapuram, Kozhikode, Chennai, Bangalore, Coimbatore, Mumbai Bombay, New Delhi, Mangalore, and Kolkata.
- Alappuzha district is well-connected by National Highway 47.Kochi,, Kollam, Thiruvananthapuram, Kozhikode, Chennai, Bangalore, Coimbatore are well connected by road to Alappuzha.

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is also called as Vembanad Bird Sanctuary , it is located in Kumarakom in Kottayam District of Kerala . national park lies on the bank of Vembanad Lake that’s why it is also called by its name. The Sanctuary is an excellent place for bird watcher as it is home to numerous migratory birds an ornithologist’s paradise.

Sprawling over 14 acres of land on bank of the Kavanar River , it is one of the most famous tourist spot in Kumarakom . Kumarakom are also famous for its backwaters and one can explore the sanctuary through backwater cruises also . Birds like waterfowl, cuckoo, owl, egret, heron, cormorant, moorhen, darter, Brahminy kite and the duck, as well as the migratory Siberian crane. Parrot, teal, lark, flycatcher can be spotted here.

How to reach?
- Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport about 85 Km away. Trivandrum Airport is about 150 Km from Here.
- Alappuzha Railway Station is the nearest Station from Here.
- It can be reached only via boats there are no road connection to here. It takes hour and half drive by motor boat or 30 minutes by speed boat from Alappuzha town. From Kumarakom it can be reached via boats in 40-50 minutes approx.

Krishnapuram Palace Museum

Krishnapuram Palace is a Museum located in Kayamkulam near Allepey in Alappuzha district in Kerala State of south India The Krishnapuram Palace was built during the reign of Marthanda Varma, a Travancore king. The palace itself was built to showcase the traditional style of architecture native to the land. The Palace is noted for its characteristic style of Kerala Architecture such as the gabled roofs, narrow corridors, dorma windows, pent roofs and so on. It is also famous for a large pond within the palace complex.The unknown artist’s masterful strokes continue to delight and marvel viewers.There are no authentic records stating the period of the palace’s construction.

Visiting Time: 9.00 am to 5.00 pm except Monday

How to reach?
- The Kollam railway station is around 39 kilometers from this museum.
- The nearest airport is the Thiruvananthapuram International Airport which is located at a distance of 103 kilometers.
- The museum is easily accessible by roads as Kayamkulam is about 47 km from Alappuzha is well connected by a wide network of motorable roads.
